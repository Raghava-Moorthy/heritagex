Description

Chrysopogon zizanioides, commonly known as vetiver and khus, is a perennial bunchgrass of the family Poaceae. Vetiver is most closely related to Sorghum but shares many morphological characteristics with other fragrant grasses.




USES & BENEFITS



Vetiver is sometimes applied directly to the skin for relieving stress, as well as for emotional traumas and shock, lice, and repelling insects.

Applying the oil from the root of Vetiver on the body gives a refreshing and cooling effect to our body.

Drinking this water eliminates digestive problems and improves the metabolic process.

Vetiver water can be used to improve heart health and control blood pressure.

Vetiver root mixed with turmeric and applied on the skin can eliminate skin ailments and cure excessive sweating and odor.
